vultures

Outside the vultures circle.
The smell of death calls them.
Their nostrils flare in delight,
ballpoints craning,
bow ties ruffled in expectation.


Beside me Johnny rots.
He died on Tuesday
in his office chair.
We try to ignore him:
attention seeker


Yesterday his wife called.
Had we seen him around lately?
She'll pick him up tomorrow,
schedule permitting.
I hope she does. He's a distraction:
I can't stand that bloody tie.
