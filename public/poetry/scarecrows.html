scarecrows

We forge a path
Through bleached grey streets
Ignoring wind blasted alleys

Destination nowhere

In shattered dialects
We say and then disconnect
The speaker cord with
An electric squeal
In starched white shirts
And stark grey suits
As disaffecting scarecrows
Failing to scare the crows
Which flutter around
Squawking in electric blings!
To beepers, emails,
Cell-phone rings


And gradually
The clock on the wall points at
The appointed hour
And the out tray
Boasts its contents to the
Disinherited in tray
And the crows flutter angrily
- the scarecrow moves


The last window is shut
And a sigh
from some wind-blasted alley
is cut off in mid whisper
