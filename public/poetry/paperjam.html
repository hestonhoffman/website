paper jam

A scream from the neighbouring cubicle
I throw my crumpled paper basketball
John from accounting falls from his chair
The paper ball flies through my miniature hoop
John is squirming on the floor now
I remember what I was doing
And quickly complete the claim sheet
Something is emerging from John's chest
It pushes up underneath his shirt
I start my next claim sheet
A button pops off and hits me on the head
John screams again
"Would you keep it down!" someone shouts
John is out of breath now
But he still flaps his hands around
A solid plastic corner has emerged
I've made a mistake on the claim sheet
A gargling hiss from John
Who has squirmed across the isle
And is holding on to my leg
I white-out the mistake -
Noticing a blot of ink on my finger
Shaped almost like a butterfly
Which, after close scrutiny, I rub off
John rolls over on his back
A small compact printer
Tears itself free from his chest
I'm feeling a little thirsty
The printer hops down the corridor
I walk to the water-cooler
The printer disappears around the corner
A satisfying gargle as my paper cup is filled.
